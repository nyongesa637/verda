# Verda Trademark Policy

> Last updated: 2026-05-12

"Verda", the Verda wordmark, and the Verda mark / logo (the **Verda
Marks**) are trademarks of the Verda project. This policy explains what
you may and may not do with the Verda Marks.

The AGPL-3.0 license under which this code is distributed grants you
broad rights to **the source code and binaries**. It does **not** grant
you any rights to the Verda Marks. Trademark rights are governed
separately by this policy and by applicable trademark law.

## What you may do without asking

You may use the Verda Marks to:

- **Refer to the project truthfully.** "I built X on top of Verda" /
  "Compatible with Verda" / "Forked from Verda" — nominative fair use is
  fine.
- **Distribute unmodified copies** of Verda under the project's name,
  with the original LICENSE, NOTICE, and TRADEMARK files preserved.
- **Show the mark in editorial / educational contexts** — articles,
  blog posts, conference talks, screenshots, video tutorials.
- **Link to the project** at https://github.com/nyongesa637/verda using
  the mark as a hyperlink.

In all cases the mark must remain unmodified — same colours, same
proportions, no recolouring or stretching — and you may not imply
endorsement or affiliation that doesn't exist.

## What requires prior written permission

You may **not** do the following without first obtaining written
permission from the project maintainer:

1. **Use the Verda Marks to brand a fork.** If you modify Verda and host
   it (or distribute a binary), pick a different name and mark for your
   distribution. You are free to write "based on Verda" in the
   description, but the *product name* must be yours.
2. **Use the Verda Marks for any commercial product or service** that
   is not the upstream Verda project. This includes hosted SaaS,
   consultancy branding, courses, certifications, merch, domain names,
   social-media handles, and any "Verda Pro / Verda Cloud / Verda
   Enterprise" naming.
3. **Register a Verda-related trademark or domain** in any
   jurisdiction.
4. **Combine the Verda Marks with another mark** in a way that suggests
   a joint product (e.g. "Verda + Acme", "AcmeVerda").
5. **Use the Verda Marks in a way that disparages the project, its
   contributors, or the human-rights defenders it serves.**

## Why this matters

Verda is built for human-rights defenders working under real
surveillance pressure. They rely on the integrity of the product they
download. If anyone could call any fork "Verda", we lose the ability to
say *that* binary, *that* hash, *that* signature is the real upstream
build. Trademark protection is the last line of defence against
malicious clones masquerading as Verda.

## How to request permission

Open an issue prefixed with `[trademark]` describing:

- who you are,
- exactly how you want to use the Verda Marks,
- a mock-up if relevant,
- and what jurisdiction.

A maintainer will reply within 30 days. Permission, if granted, will be
in writing and may carry conditions (e.g. visible disclaimers, time
limits).

## What to do if you see misuse

If you spot someone using the Verda Marks in a way this policy
forbids — especially a SaaS clone or a domain squat — please email the
address listed in `SECURITY.md` with a description and any evidence.
